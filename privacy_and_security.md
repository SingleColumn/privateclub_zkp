# Privacy & Security Overview — Proof of Funds Credential App

**Author:** Lead Developer  
**Date:** 2026-02-26  

---

## 1. Purpose

This document unifies the privacy and security architecture for the Proof of Funds Credential App.

The system is designed to:
- Allow members to demonstrate financial capability while minimizing disclosure of sensitive financial information.
- Make it difficult for members to falsely claim available funds, without bank-grade custody or escrow.

Privacy and security are treated as first-class design principles.

---

## 2. Design Principles

- **Data minimization:** Only coarse financial tiers and minimal metadata are shared.
- **Member privacy by design:** No exact balances, account numbers, bank names, or transaction history are revealed to verifiers.
- **Member-controlled sharing:** Members decide when to generate a credential, which tier to disclose, and who receives it.
- **Client integrity enforcement:** Credentials are accepted only if generated by the official app running in a non-compromised environment (policy-configurable).
- **Cryptographic binding:** Proofs are cryptographically bound to credential payloads.
- **Key isolation:** Sensitive keys never leave secure hardware storage.
- **No reliance on obscurity:** Protections do not depend on secret client logic or closed source.

---

## 3. Data Minimization by Design

The app follows strict data minimization principles:

- No exact account balances are shared with verifiers.
- No account numbers are stored or transmitted.
- No bank or custodian names are revealed to verifiers.
- No transaction history is collected or exposed.
- No financial data is transmitted to club servers.

Only the following information is included in a credential:

- Selected tier (e.g., ≥ €250k)
- Timestamp of credential generation
- Device integrity status
- Pseudonymous member identifier

---

## 4. Tier-Based Disclosure (Coarse Granularity)

The system uses predefined financial tiers rather than arbitrary thresholds. This ensures:

- Verifiers cannot "probe" for precise balances.
- Members choose what level of financial capability to disclose.
- Repeated verifications do not incrementally reveal additional financial information.

Only the selected tier is revealed — not the underlying balance.

---

## 5. Member-Controlled Sharing

Credentials are:

- Generated proactively by the member.
- Shared only with chosen recipients.
- Not publicly visible or broadcast.

Members control:

- When a credential is generated.
- Which tier is disclosed.
- Who receives the credential token.

There is no central directory of member financial tiers.

### 5.1 Screenshots and Forwarding of Credentials

- Screenshots and forwarding of a **received** credential are **discouraged** as they can weaken confidentiality expectations.
- When a receiving member:
  - Takes a screenshot of the credential view, or
  - Uses a system/share action to forward the credential,
    the app **must display a warning** that this behavior may not respect the club’s commitment to confidentiality.
- In addition, the **member who originally shared the credential** is **notified** (e.g., via in-app notification or activity log) that their credential was captured/forwarded, including at least a timestamp and the relevant recipient context where available.

---

## 6. No Central Financial Database

The club does not operate a financial database. The system does not:

- Store member balances.
- Maintain historical financial records.
- Aggregate financial data across members.

Financial data processing (in the current design stage) occurs only locally on the member's device.

---

## 7. Pseudonymous Identifiers

Credentials use pseudonymous member identifiers rather than legal names. This reduces:

- Cross-platform correlation risk.
- Accidental identity leakage.
- Exposure of sensitive personal information.

---

## 8. No Continuous Monitoring

The app does not:

- Continuously monitor accounts.
- Automatically refresh balances in the background.
- Track ongoing financial changes.

A credential reflects only the state at the moment of generation.

---

## 9. Local-Only Financial Processing

In the current architecture:

- Financial snapshot data is processed locally on the member's device.
- Only the derived tier result is used in the credential.
- Raw balance values are not embedded in shared tokens.

---

## 10. Opaque Credential Tokens

Shared credential tokens:

- Do not contain readable financial values.
- Are encoded in opaque form (e.g., base64url JSON).
- Require decoding and verification within the verifier app.

This prevents casual inspection of credential contents in messaging apps.

---

## 11. Security Architecture

### 11.1 Device & App Integrity Attestation

To reduce the risk of tampering, the app uses platform-backed integrity attestation.

**iOS — Apple App Attest**
- App generates a hardware-backed key in Secure Enclave.
- Apple signs an attestation object confirming, among other things:
  - App is the official bundle ID.
  - App binary is signed by the registered developer.
  - Key was generated in secure hardware.
- Verifier validates Apple’s signature and attestation payload.

**Android — Google Play Integrity API**
- App requests integrity token from Google.
- Token includes:
  - App integrity (official Play-signed build).
  - Device integrity (non-rooted / non-emulated).
- Token signature validated before accepting credential.

**Policy**
- Credentials generated without valid integrity attestation are either:
  - Rejected, or
  - Marked as “Untrusted environment” (configurable policy).

### 11.2 Cryptographic Key Security

**Key Storage**

- Private keys are generated and stored in:
  - Secure Enclave (iOS)
  - Android hardware-backed Keystore
- Keys are:
  - Non-exportable
  - Bound to the device
  - Protected by OS-level isolation

**Key Usage**

- Keys are used to:
  - Bind credential payloads to a device
  - Sign proof data
- Keys never leave secure hardware.
- No private keys are stored in plaintext or transmitted externally.

### 11.3 Proof Generation Model (Current Stage)

- ZKP functions are **API-structured placeholders**, not real cryptographic zero-knowledge proofs.
- Proof generation and verification are simulated using deterministic hashing logic.
- This enforces architectural separation between:
  - Member-side proving
  - Verifier-side validation

Future production versions may replace stub logic with real range proofs or SNARK-based constructions.

### 11.4 Anti-Tampering Controls

In addition to platform attestation:

- Jailbreak/root detection heuristics.
- Debugger detection.
- Emulator detection.
- Obfuscation of sensitive code paths.
- Runtime integrity checks where applicable.

These controls increase the difficulty of runtime manipulation but are not relied upon as sole trust anchors.

### 11.5 Token Security

- Credentials are encoded as opaque tokens (e.g., base64url JSON).
- Tokens do not contain readable financial information.
- Credential payload and proof are cryptographically bound together.
- Malformed or tampered tokens are rejected during verification.

---

## 12. Human-Controlled Recency Policy

Credentials include a timestamp. Rather than automatic expiration:

- Verifiers decide whether a credential is recent enough.
- Different social contexts may apply different recency expectations.

This avoids unnecessary background data refreshes while maintaining flexibility.

---

## 13. Known Privacy & Security Tradeoffs

The system intentionally reveals:

- That a member meets a selected financial tier.
- The timestamp of generation.

It does not attempt to hide:

- That the member chose to share a credential.
- The general magnitude category (tier) of funds.

Security limitations include:

- Highly skilled reverse engineering on jailbroken/rooted devices.
- Runtime hooking by sophisticated attackers.
- Temporary balance manipulation (“borrowed funds”).
- Credential forwarding to third parties (partially mitigated by warnings and notifications).

The system is designed to:

- Enable social trust signaling while minimizing exposure of sensitive financial information.
- Stop casual fraud and deter moderate technical abuse.
- Raise the cost of deception.

---

## 14. Open Source Considerations

If the app is open source:

- Members can verify that no hidden data collection exists.
- Members can confirm that balances are not transmitted externally.
- Members can inspect how tier selection and integrity checks are implemented.

Security remains anchored in:

- Platform signatures
- Hardware-backed keys
- Apple/Google attestation signatures

Security and privacy protections do not rely on code secrecy. Modified builds will fail integrity validation.
